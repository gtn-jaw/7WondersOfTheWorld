Landmarks Information

Landmark Interesting Fact
Christ the Redeemer The statue was struck by lightning in 2014.
Great Wall of China It was originally built to protect against invasions.
Petra Some of the city was carved directly from the rock.
Colosseum The Colosseum could be flooded for mock naval battles.
Chichén Itzá The pyramid casts a shadow of a serpent during the equinox.
Machu Picchu Its location was a well-kept secret until its rediscovery in 1911.
Taj Mahal There are myths that Shah Jahan planned to build a black Taj Mahal.

Key Facts

  • Christ the Redeemer: The statue's right thumb was damaged in a storm.
  • Great Wall of China: It is made up of various materials, including stone, brick, and tamped earth.
  • Petra: It was rediscovered by the Swiss explorer Johann Ludwig Burckhardt in 1812.
  • Colosseum: Over 500,000 people and over a million wild animals were killed in the arena.
  • Chichén Itzá: The site also contains a large cenote (sacred well).
  • Machu Picchu: The city is located at an altitude of approximately 2,430 meters (7,970 feet).
  • Taj Mahal: It took around 20 years and 20,000 workers to complete the construction.
