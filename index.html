Christ the Redeemer

Standing tall atop the Corcovado Mountain in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil, Christ the Redeemer is a colossal statue of Jesus Christ. Completed in 1931, the statue is 30 metres tall (not including its 7,92 meter pedestal) and its outstretched arms span 28 meters. It has become a global symbol of Christianity and a cultural icon of Brazil.

Great Wall of China

This awe-inspiring structure stretches over 23000km and was originally built to protect Chinese states from invasions. The Great Wall is not just a wall but a series of fortifications, incorporating watchtowers, battlements and barracks, reflecting the incredible engineering prowess of ancient China.

Petra

Nestled in the rugged desert canyons of Jordan, Petra is an ancient city carved directly into the pink sandstone cliffs. Known as the "Rose City," it served as the capital of the Nabataean Kingdom and is famous for its rock-cut architecture and water conduit system. The most iconic structure is Al-Khazneh, or "The Treasury," with its ornate, towering façade.

Colosseum

This iconic Roman amphitheater, located in the heart of Rome, Italy, is one of the greatest works of Roman engineering and architecture. Completed in AD 80, the Colosseum could hold up to 80,000 spectators and hosted gladiatorial contests, public spectacles, and other entertainment. Despite centuries of natural disasters and neglect, it remains a powerful symbol of ancient Rome's grandeur.

Chichén Itzá

A UNESCO World Heritage site, Chichén Itzá is a large pre-Columbian archaeological site in Mexico's Yucatán Peninsula. The most famous structure is El Castillo, a step-pyramid dedicated to the Mayan deity Kukulcan. The site also includes the Great Ball Court, Temple of the Warriors, and the Sacred Cenote, highlighting the sophisticated urban planning and astronomical knowledge of the Maya civilization.

Taj Mahal

This stunning white marble mausoleum in Agra, India, was commissioned by Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal. Completed in 1653, the Taj Mahal is an enduring symbol of love and an architectural masterpiece, known for its intricate marble inlays, symmetrical gardens, and reflecting pool that enhances its beauty.
